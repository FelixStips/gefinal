Overview

The experiment consists of two parts. This set of instructions describes the rules for part 1. After {{ shock_after_rounds }} round(s), there will be another short set of instructions to explain the rules for part 2.

There will be {{ players_in_your_group }} participants in your group. Of these, {{ employers_in_your_group }} participant(s) will be firms and {{ workers_in_your_group }} participant(s) will be workers.

You will take the role of {{ player.participant.string_role }}. You will keep this role for the entire course of the experiment.

In order to assure anonymity you have been randomly assigned an ID. You and other participants will keep this ID throughout the entire game. Your ID number is {{ player.participant.playerID }}.


Market stage

Each round has two stages, the market stage and the production stage. Here we will describe the market stage.

During the market phase, firms can submit public trading offers to workers. An offer consists of a wage and a desired effort level. The wage offer must be a number between 0 and 100,

0 ≤ wage ≤ 100.

The desired effort level must be a number between 1 and 10,

1 ≤ desired effort ≤ 10.

If firms send an offer, it will be visible to everybody in the market. Workers can accept one wage offer per round. Firms can employ up to three workers per round.

The market stage ends when all workers have accepted an offer or when the time limit of {{ market_time}} seconds is reached. Firms have no obligation to submit a trading offer, and workers have no obligation to accept a trading offer during the market stage.


Production Stage

Following the market phase, all workers who concluded a work contract, agreement then determine which effort level they will supply to their corresponding firms. The desired effort by the firm is not binding for the worker. The worker can choose the exact effort desired by the firm, but also a higher or lower effort.

In order to choose the effort level, the worker must enter the value for in the field “Please choose an effort level:” and press the “next” button to confirm the choice. As long as the worker has not pressed “next” he or she can alter the choice.
The effort level must be an integer between 1 and 10,

1 ≤ actual effort ≤ 10.

Workers pay an effort cost for each unit of effort they exert. The table below shows the effort cost for each effort level, for example if a worker chooses effort level 3, he will pay 2 points.

How is the workers' income calculated?

The income of a worker is determined by the wage offer of the firm and the effort level chosen by the worker. In a round where was employed, the income is calculated as the difference between the wage and the cost of effort:

Income = wage - cost of effort

Example: if a worker received a wage of 50 and provided 8 units of effort, the income would be 50 - 12 = 38.

In a round where the worker has not accepted any wage offer, he or she will receive unemployment insurance of {{ worker_outside_option }} points.


How is the employers' income calculated?

The income of a firm is determined by the number of contracts, the total wage paid and the total effort provided by the workers.

  1. If a firm has not employed any worker during a trading phase, they will earn an income of {{ employer_outside_option }} points in this period.

  2. If a firm has employed one worker, they will gain {{ mpl1 }} times each effort unit and pay the wage. The income thus amounts to:
    • Income = {{ mpl1 }} * effort - wage
    Example: If a firm concludes one contract and the wage is {{ ex_1_wage }} and the effort level is {{ ex_1_effort }}, the income is Income = {{ mpl1 }}*{{ ex_1_effort }} - {{ ex_1_wage }} = {{ ex_1_employer_profit }}.

  3. If a firm has employed two workers, each unit of actual effort is multiplied with {{ mpl2 }} instead of {{ mpl1 }}. The value of the products in total can be higher if the firm employs two worker, but the productivity of each individual worker is lower in this case.
    With two workers The income is calculated like this:
    • Income = {{ mpl2 }} * effort 1 + {{ mpl2 }} * effort 2 - wage 1 - wage 2
    4. Example: If a firm concludes two contracts and the wages are {{ ex_2_wage_1 }} and {{ ex_2_wage_2 }} and the effort levels are {{ ex_2_effort_1 }} and {{ ex_2_effort_2 }}, the income in that round will be Income = {{ mpl2 }}*({{ ex_2_effort_1 }} + *{{ ex_2_effort_2 }}) - {{ ex_2_wage_1 }} - {{ ex_2_wage_2 }}= {{ ex_2_employer_profit }}.
